# Malaysia Income Tax: Comprehensive Research Notes

**Research date:** 2026-02-25
**Scope:** Individual income tax for Malaysia residents and non-residents (LHDN/HASiL)
**Primary official source:** [hasil.gov.my](https://www.hasil.gov.my)

## 1. Progressive Tax Brackets — Resident Individuals

### Current Rates: Assessment Years 2023, 2024, and 2025

The same rate schedule applies across YA 2023, YA 2024, and YA 2025. No changes were introduced in Budget 2025 or Budget 2026 to the brackets themselves.

| Chargeable Income (RM) | Tax on Band Floor (RM) | Rate on Excess |
|------------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| 0 – 5,000              | 0                      | 0%             |
| 5,001 – 20,000         | 0                      | 1%             |
| 20,001 – 35,000        | 150                    | 3%             |
| 35,001 – 50,000        | 600                    | 6%             |
| 50,001 – 70,000        | 1,500                  | 11%            |
| 70,001 – 100,000       | 3,700                  | 19%            |
| 100,001 – 400,000      | 9,400                  | 25%            |
| 400,001 – 600,000      | 84,400                 | 26%            |
| 600,001 – 2,000,000    | 136,400                | 28%            |
| Above 2,000,000        | 528,400                | 30%            |

**Top marginal rate: 30%** (on chargeable income above RM 2,000,000)

**Sources:**
- [HASiL — Tax Rate (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/tax-rate/)
- [PwC Worldwide Tax Summaries — Malaysia Individual Taxes on Personal Income](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malaysia/individual/taxes-on-personal-income)

### Mechanics: How Chargeable Income is Computed

```
Gross Income
- Exempt Income (see Section 7 below)
- Approved Expenses / Deductions
- Personal Reliefs
= Aggregate Income
- Approved Donations (capped at 10% of aggregate income)
= Chargeable Income
→ Apply progressive tax table above
- Tax Rebates (RM400 / RM800 if chargeable income ≤ RM35,000)
- Zakat Rebate (capped at tax payable)
= Net Tax Payable
```

### Non-Resident Flat Rate

Non-residents pay a **flat 30%** on all Malaysia-sourced income. They are NOT entitled to:
- Any personal reliefs
- Any tax rebates
- Graduated rate benefit

**Source:** [HASiL — Non-Resident](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/non-resident/)

### Special Preferential Rate: 15% Flat

A 15% flat rate applies for a five-year period to:
1. Knowledge workers employed by designated companies in **Iskandar Malaysia** (Johor)
2. Approved residents under the **Returning Expert Programme (REP)**
3. C-Suite executives at companies awarded **PENJANA** relocation incentives
4. Knowledge workers in the **Forest City Special Financial Zone** (proposed)

**Source:** [PwC — Malaysia Individual Taxes on Personal Income](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malaysia/individual/taxes-on-personal-income)

## 3. Deductions

### 3.1 Zakat — Special Mechanics (Rebate, Not Deduction)

Zakat is handled differently from reliefs and deductions. It is a **rebate** (credit against tax payable), not a deduction against income.

**Mechanics:**
1. Compute chargeable income (after all reliefs)
2. Compute tax on chargeable income (using the progressive table)
3. Subtract the zakat amount as a **direct credit against the tax payable**
4. The rebate is capped at the total tax payable for that year — excess zakat cannot be refunded or carried forward
5. Zakat must be paid to an official state zakat authority (not given directly to individuals)
6. A receipt from the zakat authority in the individual's name is required
7. The zakat paid must be in the same Year of Assessment as the tax year being filed

**Example:** If tax payable is RM5,000 and zakat paid is RM3,000, net tax payable = RM2,000. If zakat paid is RM6,000, net tax payable = RM0 (the extra RM1,000 is forfeited — no refund, no carryforward).

**Sources:**
- [HASiL — Rebates (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/rebates/)
- [HASiL — TAX DEDUCTION, RELIEF AND REBATE (PDF)](https://phl.hasil.gov.my/pdf/pdfam/007a.pdf)

### 3.2 Approved Donations

Donations and contributions to approved institutions/organisations (approved under Section 44(6) of the Income Tax Act 1967) are **deductible from aggregate income**, subject to a cap of **10% of aggregate income**.

**Types of approved donations:**
- Cash donations to government, state government, local authorities
- Donations to LHDN-approved charities and foundations (holding a valid Section 44(6) approval letter)
- Donations to public universities, research institutions, and approved sports bodies
- Anti-corruption programme donations (approved by MACC) — introduced in Budget 2026

**Documentation required:**
- Official receipt from the recipient organisation
- Receipt must include the organisation's LHDN/IRBM registration number, donation amount, and date
- LHDN recognises two types of receipts: Financial Receipt (Kew-38) and KPHDN approval receipts

**Cap:** Deductible donations cannot exceed 10% of the individual's aggregate income for that year.

**Sources:**
- [HASiL — Explanation of Donation Receipts (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/institutionsorganizationsfunds-primarily-is-not-for-profit/donation-receipts/explanation-of-the-use-of-donation-receipts/)
- [Ringgit Plus — Income Tax Quick Guide: Donations](https://ringgitplus.com/en/blog/income-tax/income-tax-malaysia-quick-guide-to-tax-deductions-for-donations-and-gifts.html)

### 3.3 Employment Expenses

Employees may deduct expenses "incurred wholly and exclusively in the performance of their duties." Private expenses and depreciation are excluded. Professional association subscriptions are deductible. This deduction is rarely significant for most salaried employees.

### 3.4 Tax Rebates (Direct Reduction Against Tax Payable)

These reduce tax payable directly (not taxable income):

| Rebate | Amount (RM) | Condition |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Individual rebate | 400 | Chargeable income ≤ RM35,000 |
| Spouse rebate | 400 | Chargeable income ≤ RM35,000; assessed jointly or spouse assessed separately |
| Zakat / Fitrah | Actual amount paid | Capped at tax payable; excess forfeited |
| Departure levy | RM8–RM150 | For Umrah / Hajj travel; cabin-class dependent |

**Source:** [HASiL — Rebates (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/rebates/)

## 4. PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) — Monthly Tax Deduction

### What It Is

PCB (Potongan Cukai Bulanan) is also called MTD (Monthly Tax Deduction). It is the employer's mandatory withholding of estimated income tax from an employee's monthly salary. The employer remits this to HASiL by the 15th of the following month.

**Source:** [HASiL — MTD Payment (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/employers/mtd-payment/)

### How the Amount Is Calculated

The PCB amount is computed using HASiL's official formula, which:
1. Estimates the employee's total annual taxable income (based on current monthly salary extrapolated to full year)
2. Subtracts applicable reliefs (as declared by the employee on form CP34A / TP1)
3. Applies the progressive tax table to get estimated annual tax
4. Divides by the number of remaining pay periods in the year

Employers use either:
- The official e-Jadual PCB calculator on HASiL's e-CP39 system, or
- Payroll software that implements the same algorithm

### Is PCB the Final Tax?

**Starting from YA 2014, PCB can serve as the final tax** — if and only if:
- The employee's only income is employment income subject to PCB
- The employer was provided accurate relief information (via TP1/CP34A form)
- The employee does not have other income sources to declare

In this case, the employee is not required to file an annual return (Form BE). However, filing is still recommended if:
- The employee wants to claim additional reliefs not communicated to the employer
- PCB was over-withheld and a refund is expected
- The employee has other income (rental, freelance, dividends above RM100,000, etc.)

### Annual Filing Adjustment

For employees who DO file the annual return (Form BE, due by April 30 each year for non-business income):
- If total PCB paid > actual tax liability: HASiL refunds the difference (typically 30–90 days after e-filing)
- If total PCB paid < actual tax liability: the balance must be paid upon filing

**Sources:**
- [Talenox — Introduction to MTD/PCB Part 1](https://blog.talenox.com/intro-mtd-pcb-part-1/)
- [ANC Group — Monthly Tax Deduction as Final Tax](https://ancgroup.biz/monthly-tax-deduction-malaysia-final-tax-pcb-rules/)

## 5. Tax Residency

### Primary Test: 182-Day Rule

An individual is a **tax resident** of Malaysia if they are physically present in Malaysia for **182 days or more** during the calendar year. The 182 days do not need to be consecutive — they can be accumulated over the year.

**Day counting:** Every day or part of a day in Malaysia counts, including arrival and departure days.

### Alternative Tests (Where Primary Test Is Not Met)

An individual may still qualify as a tax resident under any of these secondary tests:

**Test 2 — 90 Days + 3-of-4 Prior Years:**
Present in Malaysia for at least 90 days in the current year AND was a tax resident in at least 3 of the preceding 4 years.

**Test 3 — Prior Year Link:**
Was a tax resident in the immediately preceding year AND will be a tax resident in the immediately following year.

**Test 4 — Temporary Absence:**
Was otherwise present for 182 days but for short absences (illness, or absence connected to the period of presence), still treated as resident.

**Source:** [OECD — Malaysia Residency for Tax Purposes](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/topics/policy-issue-focus/aeoi/malaysia-residency.pdf)

### Resident vs. Non-Resident: Key Differences

| Feature | Resident | Non-Resident |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Tax rate | Progressive 0%–30% | Flat 30% |
| Personal reliefs | Eligible | Not eligible |
| Tax rebates | Eligible (if chargeable income ≤ RM35,000) | Not eligible |
| Zakat rebate | Eligible | Not eligible |
| Foreign-sourced income | See Section 7 | Malaysian-source only taxed at 30% |
| Employment ≤60 days | Subject to 30% flat (unless DTA applies) | Exempt if ≤60 days |

### Non-Resident 60-Day Exemption

Non-residents employed in Malaysia for **60 days or fewer** in a calendar year are **exempt from income tax** on that employment income (unless they are directors, public entertainers, or provide technical services — those are always taxable). Treaty rates may also apply under Malaysia's Double Taxation Agreements.

**Source:** [HASiL — Non-Resident (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/non-resident/)

## 6. Capital Gains Tax

Malaysia has two capital gains regimes. These are separate taxes:

### 6.1 Real Property Gains Tax (RPGT) — Applies to ALL

RPGT is a long-established tax on gains from disposal of real property and real property company shares. It applies to individuals, companies, and non-residents.

**RPGT Rates (effective from 2022 onwards):**

| Holding Period | Malaysian Citizens and PRs | Malaysian Companies / Trustees | Non-Citizens and Foreign Companies |
|----------------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------|
| Year 1–3       | 30%                       | 30%                           | 30%                                 |
| Year 4         | 20%                       | 20%                           | 30%                                 |
| Year 5         | 15%                       | 15%                           | 30%                                 |
| Year 6+        | **0% (Nil)**              | 10%                           | **10%**                             |

**Key RPGT Exemptions:**
- Every individual (citizen, PR, or foreigner) is automatically exempt on the greater of RM10,000 or 10% of the chargeable gain per disposal
- Malaysian citizens and PRs: one lifetime exemption on disposal of a private residence (Form CKHT 1A Lampiran 3 required)
- Family transfers between spouses, parents–children, grandparents–grandchildren (transferor must be Malaysian citizen): fully exempt

**Source:** [HASiL — RPGT Rates (official)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/rpgt/real-property-gains-tax-rpgt-rates/)

### 6.2 Capital Gains Tax (CGT) on Shares — Introduced 1 March 2024

A new CGT regime was introduced through the Finance (No. 2) Act 2023, effective 1 March 2024. **Critically, this CGT applies only to corporate entities — not individuals.**

**Entities subject to CGT:**
- Companies
- Limited liability partnerships (LLPs)
- Trust bodies (unit trusts, etc.)
- Co-operative societies

**Individuals are NOT subject to CGT.**

**Assets covered (for corporate entities):**
- Unlisted shares in Malaysian-incorporated companies
- Shares in foreign companies deriving value (≥75%) from Malaysian real property

**CGT Rates (for corporate entities):**
| Acquisition Date | Rate |
|------------------|------|
| Before 1 Jan 2024 | 10% on gains OR 2% on gross proceeds (disposer's choice) |
| On or after 1 Jan 2024 | 10% on gains |
| Foreign capital assets (all types) | Prevailing corporate tax rate (~24%) when gains received in Malaysia |

**Filing:** Each disposal reported separately in a CGT Declaration Form within 60 days of disposal.

**Key Exemptions (corporate):**
- IPOs approved by Bursa Malaysia (gazetted October 2024, effective 1 March 2024–31 December 2028)
- Internal group restructuring exercises
- Venture capital-related investments
- Unit trusts: exempt through 31 December 2028

**Listed shares on Bursa Malaysia:** Gains from selling listed Bursa shares remain **fully exempt** for all entities including companies, and especially for individual investors.

**Sources:**
- [EY — Malaysia's New CGT Regime: Your Questions Answered](https://www.ey.com/en_my/technical/tax-alerts/malaysias-new-capital-gains-tax-regime-your-questions-answered)
- [KPMG — Capital Gains Tax Comes to Malaysia (PDF)](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/my/pdf/kpmg-capital-gain-tax.pdf)
- [HASiL — CGT Guidelines for Unlisted Shares (PDF)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/media/rgndkcuh/20250721-guidelines-on-capital-gains-tax-for-unlisted-shares.pdf)

### 6.3 Dividend Tax (New from YA 2025)

While not formally a capital gains tax, a new **2% tax on dividend income exceeding RM100,000** was introduced in Budget 2025, effective YA 2025.

**Mechanics:**
- Applies to dividend income distributed by Malaysian resident companies
- Only the amount exceeding RM100,000 is taxed at 2%
- Applies to resident individuals, non-resident individuals, and individuals holding shares through nominees
- Example: RM120,000 dividends received → tax = 2% × RM20,000 = RM400

**Exempt from the 2% dividend tax:**
- Dividends from companies with pioneer status or reinvestment allowances
- Dividends from shipping companies, cooperatives, and closed-end funds
- Distributions from EPF (KWSP), LTAT, ASNB (Amanah Saham Nasional)
- Distributions by any unit trust fund
- Dividends from Labuan entities

**Sources:**
- [KPMG — Dividend Tax on Individual Shareholders (PDF)](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/my/pdf/kpmg-2025-budget-dividend-tax-on-individual-shareholders.pdf)
- [Azmi and Associates — Malaysia's New 2% Dividend Tax](https://www.azmilaw.com/insights/malaysias-new-2-dividend-tax/)

## 7. Foreign-Sourced Income — Tax Treatment

### General Rule (Post-2022)

Malaysia shifted from a territorial tax system to a **modified worldwide system** effective 1 January 2022. Resident individuals are now taxed on foreign-sourced income received in Malaysia.

However, a broad exemption order is in force:

**Exemption: 1 January 2022 to 31 December 2036**
Foreign-sourced income received in Malaysia by resident individuals is **exempt from tax**, subject to conditions. The exemption applies to all classes of individual income (employment, dividends, interest, rental, royalties, pensions) received from outside Malaysia, EXCEPT for income received through a partnership business in Malaysia.

This exemption is currently extended and in force through 2036 under the Finance Act 2024.

**Source:** [taxathand — Finance Act 2024: Foreign Income Exemption Extended](https://www.taxathand.com/article/38335/Malaysia/2025/Finance-Act-2024-enacted-tax-exemption-for-foreign-income-of-individuals-extended)

### Interest Income

Bank interest received from Malaysian financial institutions is **exempt** for residents. Non-resident individuals receiving interest from Malaysian banks are also exempt.

### Dividends from Malaysian Companies

Under Malaysia's single-tier dividend system (in place since 2008), dividends paid by Malaysian resident companies are generally **exempt in the hands of shareholders**. The company has already paid tax on its profits. The new 2% tax (YA 2025+) applies only on the excess above RM100,000 (see Section 6.3).

**Source:** [PwC — Malaysia Individual Income Determination](https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/malaysia/individual/income-determination)

## 8. Special Schemes and Tax Incentives

### 8.1 Returning Expert Programme (REP)

**Administered by:** TalentCorp Malaysia
**Target:** Malaysian citizens who have been working and living abroad, to encourage their return

**Tax incentive:** A flat income tax rate of **15%** on employment income for **5 consecutive years** from the year the individual returns to Malaysia and commences qualifying employment.

**Eligibility criteria:**
- Must be a Malaysian citizen
- Must have been residing outside Malaysia AND employed abroad continuously for at least 3 years at the time of application
- Must not have earned employment income in Malaysia continuously for the 3 years prior to application
- Application must be submitted to TalentCorp BEFORE returning to Malaysia
- No outstanding scholarship bond or government loan
- Must take up employment with an approved company in Malaysia (subject to TalentCorp approval)

**Application window:** Extended — applications accepted from 1 January 2024 to 31 December 2027

**Opting in:** The 15% flat rate is optional. Applicants may choose to use the normal progressive rates instead (e.g., if their income is low enough that the normal rate is lower than 15%).

**Sources:**
- [TalentCorp — REP Official Page](https://www.talentcorp.com.my/our-initiatives/for-professionals/rep/)
- [EY — Application for Tax Incentive under REP](https://www.ey.com/en_my/technical/tax-alerts/application-for-tax-incentive-under-the-returning-expert-program)

### 8.2 Knowledge Worker Incentives — Iskandar Malaysia

Knowledge workers employed with designated companies in the Iskandar Malaysia special economic zone (southern Johor) qualify for a **15% flat income tax rate** on qualifying employment income. This is a separate scheme from REP.

### 8.3 MM2H (Malaysia My Second Home) — Retirees and Long-Stay Foreigners

The MM2H programme allows long-term residence visas. Tax treatment for MM2H holders:

- Foreign-sourced income (pension, dividends, interest, rental income from abroad) remitted to Malaysia: **exempt** under the 2022–2036 blanket FSI exemption
- Malaysian-sourced employment income: taxed at resident rates (if the person qualifies as a tax resident)
- Malaysian-sourced rental income, business income: taxed at resident rates

**Practical implication for retirees:** A foreign retiree who is a Malaysian tax resident via the 182-day rule and has only foreign pension income and investment returns remitted from abroad will effectively pay **zero Malaysian income tax** on those funds under the current FSI exemption (valid through 2036). Any Malaysian-sourced income (e.g., local property rental) would still be taxable.

**Source:** [MISHU — MM2H Tax Exemptions](https://mishu.my/blog/visa/mm2h-tax-exemptions/)

### 8.4 Iskandar Malaysia — Forest City Special Financial Zone

For the Forest City Special Financial Zone (Johor, near the Singapore border), there are proposed preferential rates for qualifying financial services workers. As of the research date, details are still being finalised and it is treated as a "proposed" incentive.

## 9. EPF Contribution Rates (Reference for Tax Calculation)

EPF (Employees Provident Fund / KWSP) contributions are relevant because they flow through to tax reliefs (RM4,000 cap on the EPF portion of the combined EPF + life insurance relief).

| Category | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Monthly wages ≤ RM5,000 | 11% | 13% |
| Monthly wages > RM5,000 | 11% | 12% |

No salary ceiling for EPF purposes (unlike Singapore's CPF OW ceiling). However, the tax relief cap on EPF contributions is RM4,000 regardless of actual contributions.

Foreign employees: Previously optional, now mandatory EPF contributions apply for certain categories of foreign workers.

**Source:** [KWSP — Employer Mandatory Contribution](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/employer/responsibilities/mandatory-contribution)

For completeness, when a Malaysian entity pays certain types of income to non-residents, withholding tax (WHT) applies at source:

| Income Type | Standard WHT Rate | Notes |
|-------------|------------------|-------|
| Interest | 15% | Exempt if paid by Malaysian commercial/merchant bank |
| Royalties | 10% | Final tax |
| Technical / management fees | 10% | On payments for services performed in Malaysia |
| Dividends (domestic) | 0% (single-tier) + 2% on excess RM100,000 from YA 2025 | Under single-tier system |

DTA (Double Taxation Agreement) rates may reduce these where applicable. Malaysia has DTAs with many countries including Singapore.

## 12. Key Differences vs. Singapore (IRAS)

For context when migrating the FIRE planner:

| Feature | Malaysia (LHDN) | Singapore (IRAS) |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Top marginal rate | 30% (above RM 2M) | 24% (above SGD 1M) |
| Dividend tax | 0% under single-tier (+ 2% above RM100k from 2025) | 0% (single-tier; no additional tax) |
| Capital gains tax | None for individuals; RPGT for property; CGT for corporates on unlisted shares | None |
| Foreign income | Exempt 2022–2036 for individuals | Exempt (territorial basis) |
| Key retirement savings | EPF (KWSP) | CPF (OA / SA / MA / RA) |
| Property tax on gains | RPGT (0% after 6 years for citizens/PRs) | None (except seller's stamp duty within 3 years) |
| Filing currency | Ringgit Malaysia (RM / MYR) | Singapore Dollar (SGD) |
