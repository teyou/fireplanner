## 1. Contribution Rates by Age Bracket

### 1.1 Malaysian Citizens and Permanent Residents

Contributions are calculated using the **Third Schedule of the EPF Act 1991**, which provides banded fixed-RM contribution amounts for wages up to RM20,000/month. The percentage rates below are the effective rates that the banded amounts approximate. For wages above RM20,000/month, contributions are calculated directly as a percentage.

| Age Group | Employee Rate | Employer Rate (Wages <= RM5,000) | Employer Rate (Wages > RM5,000) |
|-----------|--------------|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Below 60  | 11%          | 13%                              | 12%                              |
| 60 and above (Malaysian citizen) | 0% | 4% | 4% |
| 60 and above (PR) | 5.5% | 6.5% (wages <= RM5,000) | 6% (wages > RM5,000) |

**Key rule:** The RM5,000 salary threshold determines employer contribution rate (13% vs 12%), not employee rate.

Sources:
- [KWSP: Mandatory Contribution](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/employer/responsibilities/mandatory-contribution)
- [KWSP: Contribute as a Member](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/overview/contribution)
- [KWSP: EPF Act 1991 Third Schedule](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/epf-act-1991-third-schedule)
- [KWSP: Third Schedule PDF](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/documents/d/guest/jadual-ketiga-bi-pdf-1)

### 1.2 Non-Malaysian Citizens

**Registered before 1 August 1998 (legacy treatment):**
| Age Group | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|
| Below 60  | 11%          | RM5.00 flat   |
| 60 and above | 5.5%      | RM5.00 flat   |

**Registered from 1 August 1998 onwards (prior to Oct 2025):** Voluntary contributions only.

**From 1 October 2025 (new mandatory rule):** EPF contributions became mandatory for all non-Malaysian citizen employees:
- Employee: 2% of monthly wages
- Employer: 2% of monthly wages
- These rates will increase in phases over 6 years to reach parity with Malaysian citizen rates.

Sources:
- [KWSP: Non-Malaysian Citizen Employees](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/employer/responsibilities/non-malaysian-citizen-employees)
- [Lockton: Malaysia Introduces Mandatory EPF for Foreign Employees](https://global.lockton.com/us/en/news-insights/malaysia-introduces-mandatory-epf-contributions-for-foreign-employees)

### 1.3 Employee Opt-Down Option (11% to 9%)

From time to time (e.g., during COVID-19 in 2021), the Malaysian government has introduced a **temporary** reduced statutory employee contribution rate of **9%** (down from 11%) as a cash flow relief measure. This is NOT a permanent structural option.

- The 2021 reduction ran from January 2021 to December 2021 (contributions from February 2021 to January 2022).
- Employees who wished to maintain the full 11% had to submit **Borang KWSP 17A (Khas 2021)** via their employer.
- When in effect, 9% is the default statutory rate; members must actively opt up to 11%.
- As of 2025, the standard rate has reverted to 11%; no permanent 9% opt-down exists as a standing policy.

Source:
- [KWSP: Reduction of Employee Contribution Rate](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/w/reduction-of-employee-contribution-rate)

### 1.4 Voluntary / Excess Contributions (i-Topup)

**i-Topup** allows both employees and employers to contribute above the statutory rate. The elected rate becomes the new contribution rate and persists until a cancellation notice is submitted via Form KWSP 17A (AHL) / 18A (AHL).

**For self-employed and those with no fixed income:** See Section 1.5 (i-Saraan).

Sources:
- [KWSP: i-Topup (Member)](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/savings/voluntary-excess)
- [KWSP: i-Topup (Employer)](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/employer/responsibilities/contributing-more-than-the-statutory-rate)

### 1.5 i-Saraan (Voluntary Contributions for Self-Employed)

**i-Saraan** is the EPF scheme for the self-employed and those with informal income who have no mandatory EPF employer contributions.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Maximum voluntary contribution per year | RM100,000 (combined across Self Contribution, i-Saraan, and Account 1 Top-up) |
| Government incentive rate | 20% matching on annual contributions |
| Maximum annual government incentive | RM500 (requires minimum RM2,500 contributed that year) |
| Lifetime incentive cap | RM5,000 (effective from 2024, or until member turns 60, whichever comes first) |
| Tax relief available | Yes (see Section 6) |

The i-Saraan incentive cap of RM5,000 lifetime was introduced in 2024. Previously there was no lifetime cap.

Sources:
- [KWSP: i-Saraan](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/savings/i-saraan)
- [Malaysia.gov.my: EPF i-Saraan Incentive Registration](https://www.malaysia.gov.my/en/digital-services/epf-i-saraan-incentive-registration)

### 1.6 Monthly/Annual Wage Ceiling

- **No upper wage ceiling** for EPF contributions in Malaysia (unlike Singapore's CPF OW/AW caps).
- Employees earning **above RM20,000/month**: contributions calculated directly as exact percentages (11% employee, 12% or 13% employer).
- Employees earning **RM20,000/month or below**: contribution amounts determined by the Third Schedule banded table (fixed RM amounts per salary band).
- **Minimum age to contribute:** 14 years old.
- **Maximum age:** Contributions continue up to age 75.

Source:
- [KWSP: EPF Act 1991 Third Schedule](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/epf-act-1991-third-schedule)

## 2. Account Structure (3-Account System, effective 11 May 2024)

EPF restructured from a 2-account system (Account 1 / Account 2) to a **3-account system** effective 11 May 2024. The restructuring applies to all **new contributions** made from that date. Existing balances remain in their respective accounts (old Account 1 -> Akaun Persaraan, old Account 2 -> Akaun Sejahtera).

### 2.1 Akaun Persaraan (Retirement Account) - formerly Account 1

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Allocation of new contributions | 75% |
| Old Account 1 balance | Stays in Akaun Persaraan (no migration needed) |
| Purpose | Core long-term retirement savings |
| Pre-retirement withdrawal | NOT allowed for housing, education, or medical purposes |
| Pre-retirement exception | Incapacitation: full withdrawal of ALL EPF savings allowed |
| At age 55 | Consolidated into "Akaun 55" along with Akaun Sejahtera and Akaun Fleksibel |
| i-Invest | Members can invest up to 30% of the amount exceeding Basic Savings in approved unit trust funds |

### 2.2 Akaun Sejahtera (Well-being Account) - formerly Account 2

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Allocation of new contributions | 15% |
| Old Account 2 balance | Stays in Akaun Sejahtera (no migration needed) |
| Purpose | Pre-retirement life-cycle needs |
| Permitted withdrawals | Housing, Education, Health, Insurance/Takaful protection, Hajj, Age 50 |
| At age 55 | Consolidated into "Akaun 55" |

**Permitted pre-retirement withdrawals from Akaun Sejahtera:**

| Purpose | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| Housing (buy/build) | First and second residential properties; see Section 5 |
| Housing (monthly loan instalment) | Monthly withdrawal to service housing loan; see Section 5 |
| Housing (reduce/redeem loan) | Lump sum to reduce outstanding mortgage balance |
| Education | For member, spouse, children, parents at EPF-approved institutions locally or abroad |
| Health | Valid medical report from EPF-approved institution required |
| Insurance/Takaful | EPF-approved products only |
| Hajj | For pilgrimage registration |
| Age 50 | Members aged 50+ can withdraw a portion of Akaun Sejahtera savings |

### 2.3 Akaun Fleksibel (Flexible Account) - new Account 3

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Allocation of new contributions | 10% |
| Starting balance (at launch) | Zero (no transfer from old accounts at launch) |
| Members could optionally transfer | From 12 May 2024 to 31 August 2024 (initial transfer window) |
| Purpose | Any purpose; fully flexible |
| Minimum withdrawal | RM50 |
| Maximum withdrawal | Full balance (no cap on amount withdrawn) |
| Supporting documents required | None |
| Processing time | Within 3 working days of approval |
| How to withdraw | KWSP i-Akaun app, i-Akaun (Member) web portal, or EPF office |
| Eligibility | Must be below 55; must have balance >= RM50 |
| Daily limit | Withdrawals of RM50 to RM3,000 are processed automatically; larger amounts may require verification |

**Note on dividends:** Akaun Fleksibel earns the same EPF dividend rate as the other accounts. Dividends are credited based on the balance in the account.

### 3.3 How Dividends Are Calculated

EPF uses a **daily aggregate balance method**:

1. **Opening balance component:** `Opening balance (1 Jan) x Dividend rate`
2. **Monthly contribution component:** For each month, aggregate balance is calculated as:
   `(Monthly opening balance x Days in month) + (Monthly contribution x 1 day)`
3. Monthly dividend: `(Aggregate balance x Dividend rate) / 365`

Dividends are credited **annually** after declaration (typically announced in late February/early March for the prior year). Withdrawals during the year receive a pro-rated dividend based on the days the funds were held.

Source:

## 4. Withdrawal Rules

### 4.1 Age 55: Akaun 55

At age 55, the savings in all three active accounts (Akaun Persaraan, Akaun Sejahtera, Akaun Fleksibel) are **consolidated into a single "Akaun 55"**. Key rules:

- Members can withdraw **all or part** of Akaun 55 at any time after turning 55.
- No purpose restriction: withdrawals are unrestricted.
- Members who continue working after 55 will have new contributions credited to **Akaun Emas** (not Akaun 55).
- Akaun Emas funds **cannot be accessed until age 60**.

Source:
- [KWSP: Age 55 & 60 Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/life-stages/age-55-60-withdrawal)

### 4.2 Age 60: Akaun Emas

**Akaun Emas** is the account for post-55 employment contributions. At age 60:
- All savings in **Akaun 55** and **Akaun Emas** are consolidated.
- Members can opt for a **lump sum or partial withdrawal** at any time.
- EPF has confirmed: no change to lump sum withdrawal rights at ages 55 and 60.

Source:
- [KWSP: No Change for Lump Sum Withdrawals at Age 55 and 60](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/w/no-change-for-lump-sum-withdrawals-at-age-55-and-60-for-existing-epf-members-1)
- [KWSP: Introducing Akaun Emas](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/w/no-change-to-age-55-withdrawal-and-introducing-akaun-emas-for-age-60-withdrawal)

### 4.3 Age 50 Withdrawal

Members aged 50+ may withdraw a portion of their **Akaun Sejahtera** savings before reaching 55. This is for pre-retirement financial flexibility.

Source:
- [KWSP: Age 50 Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/life-stages/age-50-withdrawal)

### 4.4 Pre-Retirement Withdrawals (Akaun Sejahtera only)

All pre-retirement withdrawals (other than incapacitation) are from **Akaun Sejahtera only**. Akaun Persaraan is locked until 55.

| Type | Source | Key Rules |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Housing | Akaun Sejahtera | See Section 5 for full details |
| Education | Akaun Sejahtera | Member, spouse, children, parents; approved institutions locally/abroad; amount limited to tuition fees or Account 2 balance, whichever is lower |
| Health | Akaun Sejahtera | Medical report from EPF-approved institution required |
| Insurance/Takaful | Akaun Sejahtera | EPF-approved products only |
| Hajj | Akaun Sejahtera | For pilgrimage registration with Tabung Haji |
| Incapacitation | All accounts | Full withdrawal of ALL EPF savings; RM5,000 Incapacitation Benefit provided; requires EPF medical board assessment |

Sources:
- [KWSP: Education Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/life-stages/education-withdrawal)
- [KWSP: Incapacitation Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/healthcare/incapacitation)

### 4.5 Minimum Basic Savings (Retirement Income Benchmarks)

EPF sets a "Basic Savings" benchmark as the minimum EPF balance members should have at age 55.

**Current (2019-2025):** RM240,000 at age 55.

**New Retirement Income Adequacy (RIA) Framework** (announced December 2024, implementation from January 2026):

The RIA Framework replaces the single-tier RM240,000 target with a three-tier structure. The basic savings target will increase in RM50,000 annual increments until reaching RM390,000 in 2028.

| Tier | Savings Target at 55 | Monthly Income (approx.) | Covers |
|------|---------------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Basic | RM390,000 (by 2028) | Sufficient for basic needs | Essential retirement needs |
| Adequate | RM650,000 | ~RM2,690/month | Reasonable standard of living |
| Enhanced | RM1,300,000 | Higher | Financial security and independence |

The Belanjawanku 2024/2025 guide estimates a single elderly person needs approximately **RM2,690/month** for a reasonable retirement lifestyle (Kuala Lumpur).

Sources:
- [KWSP: EPF Releases Belanjawanku 2024/2025 and RIA Framework](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/w/epf-releases-belanjawanku-2024/2025-and-retirement-income-adequacy-framework)

## 5. Housing Withdrawal (Akaun Sejahtera)

EPF provides **six types** of housing withdrawals, all funded from Akaun Sejahtera. Eligibility requires the member to be under 55.

### 5.1 Types of Housing Withdrawal

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Buy House | Purchase of completed residential property |
| Build House | Construction of residential property on owned land |
| Reduce/Redeem Housing Loan | Lump sum to reduce or fully redeem outstanding mortgage |
| Monthly Housing Loan Instalment | Monthly withdrawal to service home loan payments |
| Flexible Housing Withdrawal | Current and future contributions to strengthen loan eligibility |
| Disaster Replacement | If property destroyed by natural disaster (e.g., flood) |

Sources:
- [KWSP: Housing Withdrawal Overview](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal)
- [KWSP: Buy House Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal/buy-house)
- [KWSP: Build House Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal/build-house)
- [KWSP: Reduce/Redeem Housing Loan Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal/reduce-housing-loan)
- [KWSP: Housing Loan Instalment Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal/loan-instalment)
- [KWSP: 6 Types of EPF Housing Withdrawals](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/w/article/epf-housing-withdrawal)

### 5.2 First Property Purchase

- Member must be **under 55** years old.
- Minimum Akaun Sejahtera balance: **RM500**.
- Property must be a **residential property in Malaysia** (no commercial, no overseas property).
- Joint withdrawal with spouse or immediate family members is permitted.
- Maximum withdrawal: the **lower of** the purchase price / outstanding loan balance, or the total balance in Akaun Sejahtera.

### 5.3 Second Property Purchase

- Allowed only if the **first property has been sold or disposed of** before applying.
- Exception: if the first property was destroyed in a natural disaster (verified), a second withdrawal is permitted without disposing of the first.
- The Buy House withdrawal (Category 1) is limited to **up to 2 residential properties** per member's lifetime.
- **Category 2 (Reduce/Redeem Loan)** is available for any residential property owned, including second and subsequent properties.

### 5.4 Monthly Housing Loan Instalment Withdrawal

- Allows monthly withdrawal from Akaun Sejahtera to service home loan repayments.
- Minimum withdrawal: **RM100/month for a minimum of 6 months**.
- Maximum: must not exceed total monthly loan instalment (shared with any joint applicant).
- Subsequent applications can be made 30 days before expiry of the current approved period.

Source:
- [KWSP: Housing Loan Instalment Withdrawal](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/house-withdrawal/loan-instalment)

## 6. EPF Tax Treatment

### 6.1 Employee Contribution Tax Relief

Under Malaysia's Income Tax Act 1967 (and administered by Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri / LHDN):

| Relief Type | Maximum Amount | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Mandatory EPF contributions (Section 49(1)(b)) | RM4,000/year | Combined with approved private retirement scheme contributions |
| Voluntary EPF contributions | RM3,000/year | Combined with life insurance / family takaful premiums |
| Combined cap (EPF + Life Insurance/Takaful) | RM7,000/year | Both mandatory and voluntary EPF can be claimed together |

**Example:** An employee contributing RM36,000 mandatory EPF (11% of high salary) can only claim RM4,000 tax relief; the remainder provides no additional relief.

A self-employed person contributing voluntarily to EPF (i-Saraan) can claim RM4,000 under Section 49(1)(b) plus RM3,000 under Section 49(1)(a) = **RM7,000 total**, subject to the combined cap with life insurance.

Sources:
- [LHDN: Tax Reliefs](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/individual/individual-life-cycle/income-declaration/tax-reliefs/)
- [CCS: Additional Tax Relief for Voluntary EPF Contributions](https://ccs-co.com/post/additional-tax-relief-is-available-for-voluntary-contributions-made-to-the-epf/)

### 6.2 Tax on EPF Dividends

**EPF dividends are fully exempt from income tax.** This applies to both Simpanan Konvensional and Simpanan Shariah dividends, regardless of the amount or the member's income level. No withholding tax. No declaration required.

Source:
- [KWSP: Benefits as a Member](https://www.kwsp.gov.my/en/member/overview/benefits)

### 6.3 Tax on Withdrawals

| Withdrawal Type | Tax Treatment |
|----------------|---------------|
| Withdrawal at age 55 or above | **Tax-free** |
| Withdrawal at age 60 (Akaun Emas) | **Tax-free** |
| Pre-retirement withdrawal (housing, education, medical) from Akaun Sejahtera | **Tax-free** |
| Incapacitation withdrawal (any age) | **Tax-free** |
| Leaving Malaysia (non-citizen departure) | Tax treatment subject to treaty / LHDN ruling |

There is no taxation on any standard EPF withdrawal for Malaysian citizens and permanent residents, whether at retirement age or as approved pre-retirement withdrawals.

### 6.4 Employer Contribution Tax Treatment

Employer EPF contributions are a **tax-deductible business expense** under Section 34(4) of the Income Tax Act 1967, subject to a cap:

- **Maximum deductible:** 19% of the employee's annual remuneration.
- This cap covers all contributions to approved provident/pension funds combined.
- Contributions above 19% of remuneration are not deductible for corporate tax purposes.
- The standard employer contribution rate is 12-13%, well within the 19% cap.

Source:
- [YYC Advisors: Employer Contributes EPF to Employee's Account](https://www.yycadvisors.com/employer-contributes-EPF-to-employees-account.html)

## 7. i-Akaun Digital Services

**i-Akaun** is KWSP's digital platform available as a mobile app and web portal (i-Akaun Member).

### 7.1 Core Features Relevant to Modeling

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Account balance view | All three accounts (Akaun Persaraan, Akaun Sejahtera, Akaun Fleksibel) visible with real-time balances |
| Contribution history | Full contribution records |
| Akaun Fleksibel withdrawal | Online withdrawal application; RM50 minimum; no docs needed; paid within 3 working days |
| Housing withdrawal | Buy/Build house, Reduce/Redeem Loan, Monthly Instalment withdrawal applications |
| Health withdrawal | Online application |
| Education withdrawal | Online application |
| i-Invest | Self-service unit trust investment from Akaun Persaraan (Account 1 equivalent) |
| Voluntary contribution | Additional contributions via i-Akaun |
| i-Akaun Secure (2025) | Biometric / single-tap transaction authorization; replaces SMS TAC codes |

### 7.2 i-Invest (Unit Trust Investment Scheme)

Available within i-Akaun, allowing eligible members to invest a portion of Akaun Persaraan savings in approved unit trust funds:

- **Eligible amount:** Up to **30%** of the amount in Akaun Persaraan **in excess of Basic Savings** (currently RM240,000).
- **Approved funds:** 189 qualifying funds from 18 Fund Management Institutions (FMIs) for 2024/2025 (from 296 total EPF-approved funds). Fund list is reviewed annually by EPF.
- **Shariah options:** 76 of the qualifying funds are Shariah-compliant (40.2%).
- **Transaction volume:** ~RM2.5 billion transacted since i-Invest launch in 2019 (as of end-2023).

## 9. Key Differences vs. Singapore CPF
